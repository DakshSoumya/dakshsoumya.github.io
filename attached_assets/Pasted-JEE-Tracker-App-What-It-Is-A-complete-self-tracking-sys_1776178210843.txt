JEE Tracker App — What It Is

A complete self-tracking system for JEE prep (Class 11 + 12), combining:

📊 Syllabus tracking
📅 Daily study logging
📈 Progress analytics
🧾 Reflection + mistakes tracking

Basically, it’s trying to turn your chaotic student life into something measurable. Bold move.

📚 1. Subject & Chapter Tracking (Core Feature)

You’ve got sections for:

Physics, Chemistry, Maths
Separate for Class 11 & 12

Each chapter includes:

✔ Subtopics checklist
Tick what you’ve studied (e.g. Kinematics → Projectile Motion)
✔ Completion tracking
Notes done
DPP/HW done
Mains PYQs
Advanced PYQs
Extra books
📊 Progress bar
Shows % completion per chapter
Status:
Not started
In progress
Done
🧮 Extra tracking
Number of revisions
Mistakes count
Doubts (text box)

👉 Translation: it forces you to confront how much you haven’t done. Very motivating. Or depressing.

📈 2. Stats & Analytics

Each subject shows:

Chapters completed
Total revisions
Total mistakes
Chapters with doubts

And then:

🟢 Overall progress bar (sidebar)
Combines everything into one %

👉 That number will either boost your ego or ruin your evening.

📅 3. Daily “Class Tracker” (Diary System)

This is where things get… intense.

🏫 Class tracking
Subject, topic, teacher
Attended:
Live
Recorded
📘 Study work checklist
Theory revised
DPP solved
PYQs practiced
Formula revision
Extra book
😶 4. Mood + Reflection

Because apparently academics alone don’t torture you enough:

Mood selection:
Excellent → Rough
Reflection boxes:
“1 mistake I must not repeat”
“Today I improved at”

👉 This is actually useful, even if it feels like therapy disguised as homework.

📝 5. Tests Tracking

If you had a test:

Name
Score
Accuracy

👉 Clean and simple. No hiding from bad scores here.

📌 6. Commitments System

You can:

Add daily goals (like “Finish Kinematics DPP”)
Mark them done
Delete them

👉 Basically a to-do list that remembers your failures.

📜 7. Notes + History
Notes:
Free writing area for the day
History:
Past entries stored
Shows:
Mood
Study work
Mistakes
Improvements
Test scores
Completed commitments

👉 So your past self can silently judge your present self.

💾 8. Smart Saving System
Everything auto-saves using browser storage
Each day is stored separately

👉 No login, no cloud, just your browser holding your academic secrets hostage.

🧩 Overall Verdict

This app is basically:

A mix of Notion + habit tracker + JEE guilt generator

👍 Strengths
Extremely structured
Covers both macro (syllabus) and micro (daily work)
Forces accountability
👎 Weaknesses
Can feel overwhelming if you don’t keep up
Manual input heavy
You might spend more time tracking than studying (classic student move)
